A washer stuck full of water at the end of a cycle is almost always a drainage problem, not an electrical one. The first thing our technicians check is the drain pump filter, which catches coins, lint, and small items — a clogged filter is the single most common cause and often the cheapest fix.

If the filter is clear, the next suspects are a failing drain pump, a kinked drain hose, or a clog further down the household drain line. We test the pump directly rather than replacing it on a guess, which keeps the repair cost down when the real issue is something simpler.
